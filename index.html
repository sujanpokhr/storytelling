The Empire’s Shadow

A Final Project in Digital Storytelling

The Script

"An empire built on order… and belief. A hero... ...chosen to protect it all. But beyond the empire's story... ...lay lives its chroniclers never spoke of. Two innocent souls... ...who believed they were safe, as long as they followed the teachings of Veer Arka. Yet their life beyond the empire's control made them a threat in the eyes of the hero they so admired. Veer Arkha and his men may have come to their village as protectors... ...but to Kiran and Mira, they were nothing less than the destroyers of their peaceful world. And though history always remembers the heroes... ...it too often forgets those left behind in the ruins."

The Protagonists & The Antagonist

Kiran & Mira

Definition: Represent the "unseen cost" of progress. They are simple, peaceful souls living outside the Empire's walls. Their innocence is their primary trait, which contrasts sharply with the violent "order" brought by the hero.

Veer Arka

Definition: The legendary hero of the Empire. He is driven by absolute belief and rigid teachings. To the Empire, he is a savior; to the village, he is an unstoppable force of destruction who cannot see the humanity in those he deems a "threat."

The Story Synopsis

Our story follows the tragic collision between imperial myth and human reality. While the Empire’s history books record a glorious expansion led by the hero Veer Arka, our animation looks behind the curtain. We witness the destruction of a peaceful village that didn't fit the Empire's mold. Through the eyes of Kiran and Mira, we experience the moment their admiration for a hero turns into the terror of facing a destroyer. The project concludes by questioning who truly writes history—the ones who survived, or the ones who won?
